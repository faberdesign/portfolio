# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, design leads, and recruiters evaluating Benedikt Faber for full-time Product Design roles (with select freelance interest), primarily in the DACH market. They land on the site looking for evidence of clear thinking under constraint, narrative structure, and the ability to translate non-digital design experience into product-shaped outcomes. Their context is fast: a 30-second scan to decide whether to read further.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio that earns the interview. It frames Benedikt as a Product Designer whose advantage is storytelling structure carried over from years in Live Communication. Success looks like: the visitor reads at least one full case study, remembers the făbĕr name hook, and replies.

## Brand Personality

Warm-confident, narrative, hands-on. Three words: composed, generous, deliberate. Voice opens with Hamburg German hospitality ("Moin") and stays first-person without being twee. Emotional register is calm but committed; nothing performative, nothing self-effacing.

## Anti-references

- The generic AI-shaped portfolio: icon-plus-heading-plus-text cards repeated endlessly, gradient mesh backgrounds, "I'm passionate about pixels" copy.
- The dribbble-flex portfolio: oversized product mockups in 3D, no thinking shown.
- The Stripe-clone editorial-magazine template (display serif italic + drop caps + ruled separators) on a brief that isn't editorial.
- Anything that looks like it could belong to any of fifty other portfolios with the name swapped.

## Design Principles

1. **Narrative before evidence.** Every section answers a question the visitor is forming, in the order they form it. The hero opens, the Latin pun lands the name, the work delivers, the about closes the loop.
2. **Restraint with one bright thing.** Cream + navy + a vermilion that carries the energy. Color isn't decoration; it's voice.
3. **Show the thinking.** Case studies prioritize the why and the trade-off over the deliverable. The work IS the storytelling argument.
4. **Honest in-progress.** Unfinished work is acknowledged, not faked. "In progress" tiles read as deliberate, not as broken-image placeholders.
5. **German hospitality.** Personal touches (Moin, the bobblehead, the Latin) make it specifically Benedikt's site, not a template wearing his name.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target across light and dark surfaces (navy footer + nav included). Keyboard navigation must work end-to-end with visible focus, since the desktop custom cursor is `cursor: none` and can mask focus state. `prefers-reduced-motion` honored across marquee, scroll-reveals, hover transforms. No color-only meaning; the vermilion accent is reinforced by typography weight and structure.
